Hi! My name is Andreas and I am a PhD Candidate at MIT advised by Professor Michael S. Triantafyllou and Professor Themis Sapsis. My primary research interests lie in deep learning, time series forecasting, and generative modeling with applications to engineering and finance.

I am leveraging deep learning techniques to develop a digital twin for risers – long, flexible underwater pipelines – vibrating constantly under the excitation of stochastic hydrodynamic loads. Driven by vortex-induced vibrations (VIV), the dynamics are nonlinear, nonstationary, and exhibit strong memory effects. In order to address those challenges, I am using transformers to model and continuously forecast the vibrations in real time.

I am also leveraging generative models to synthesize instances of multivariate VIV time-series since experiments can be not only logistically challenging but also financially prohibitive. My approach includes using Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), especially Wasserstein GANs (wGANs), Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), and Denoising-Diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs). My results indicate that generated data retain much of the intrinsic properties of the original data, and that forecasting models trained on synthetic data can accurately predict real spatio-temporal VIV.

My work in generative modeling also includes LOBSTgER (Learning Oceanic Bioecological Systems Through gEnerative Representations): an effort dedicated to generating ultra-realistic high-resolution underwater imagery to raise awareness of the marine habitats in Massachusetts and the Gulf of Maine. This effort is run in collaboration with renowned photographer Keith Ellenbogen. Using Keith’s exemplary images as training data I have been able to train a latent diffusion model to generate detailed images of species such as Lion’s Mane Jellyfish, Mola Mola, and others. We are currently advancing this work and plan to publicize it through exhibitions and articles in the near future.

As far as leveraging deep learning for financial applications, I have developed market neutral statistical arbitrage trading stategies for public equities using deep neural network architectures which have been back-tested in alternative investment markets of Southeast Asia (work done as part of Wall Street internship).

I was born and raised in Athens, Greece where I developed a passion for engineering, the marine environments, and music. My academic journey began at the University of Michigan where I graduated summa cum laude with two bachelor’s degrees, one in Mechanical Engineering and the second in Naval Architecture & Marine Engineering with a minor in Mathematics (2020). My undergraduate research was on marine renewable energy harvesting at the MRELab under the mentorship of Professor M.M. Bernitsas. I joined MIT’s MechE Department as a graduate student where I have completed an SM (2022). Over the years I have developed a strong background in mathematical modeling, numerical methods, statistics, algorithms, machine learning, optimization, and programming through coursework, research, and internships.

Academics aside, I am a violinist for the MIT Symphony Orchestra and enjoy hanging out with friends, attending concerts, and immersing myself into musical theater shows.
